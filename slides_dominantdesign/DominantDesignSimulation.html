CEO Context

  • The smartphone market in 2007 is dominated by BlackBerry's physical keyboard design and Palm's stylus-based interface. Apple must decide whether to enter this established market with a radically different touchscreen-only design.

  • Consumer behavior shows strong attachment to physical keyboards for business users, but there's growing demand for multimedia and internet capabilities that current designs struggle to deliver effectively.

CTO

Question: What technology approach should Apple take for the iPhone in 2007?


A: Develop a device with physical keyboard like BlackBerry but add touchscreen

B: Create a revolutionary full touchscreen interface without physical keyboard

CTO Information

  • Touchscreen technology exists but has never been successfully implemented as the primary interface for smartphones.
  • Current market leaders (BlackBerry, Palm) rely on physical input methods that users trust and understand.
  • Multi-touch technology is unproven in mobile devices and requires significant battery power.
  • Virtual keyboard concerns: accuracy, typing speed, user acceptance for business applications.

Answer: B - Create a revolutionary full touchscreen interface without physical keyboard

CMO

Question: How should Apple position the iPhone in the market for 2007?


A: Position as a business productivity device competing with BlackBerry

B: Market as a revolutionary consumer device that redefines the smartphone category

CMO Information

  • BlackBerry dominates the business market with 85% enterprise adoption for email and messaging.
  • Consumer smartphone adoption is only 15% - most people use basic phones for calls and text.
  • iPod success shows Apple's strength in consumer electronics and design-focused marketing.
  • Touchscreen interface requires educating consumers about entirely new interaction paradigms.

Answer: B - Market as a revolutionary consumer device that redefines the smartphone category

COO

Question: How should Apple structure iPhone manufacturing for 2007?


A: Partner with existing phone manufacturers to reduce risk

B: Build proprietary manufacturing capabilities for complete control

COO Information

  • Touchscreen manufacturing requires precision glass cutting and new assembly processes.
  • Current phone manufacturers (Motorola, Nokia) lack experience with touchscreen integration.
  • Apple has no previous experience in phone manufacturing or carrier relationships.
  • Revolutionary design requires tight integration between hardware and software teams.

Answer: B - Build proprietary manufacturing capabilities for complete control

CHRO

Question: What talent strategy should Apple pursue for iPhone development in 2007?


A: Hire mobile phone industry veterans from Nokia and Motorola

B: Recruit software engineers and user interface designers from outside telecom

CHRO Information

  • Traditional phone industry talent is focused on physical keyboards and T9 input methods.
  • Apple's Mac and iPod teams have experience with intuitive user interfaces and design.
  • Touchscreen interface requires completely new thinking about human-computer interaction.
  • Industry veterans may resist radical departures from established phone design paradigms.

Answer: B - Recruit software engineers and user interface designers from outside telecom

CFO

Question: How should Apple price the iPhone for launch in 2007?


A: Price competitively with BlackBerry ($200-300) to gain market share

B: Price as premium device ($500-600) to establish new category

CFO Information

  • iPhone development costs: $150M R&D investment over 3 years
  • Manufacturing costs estimated at $280 per unit due to touchscreen technology
  • BlackBerry average selling price: $250 with established enterprise market
  • iPod success shows consumers will pay premium for superior design and user experience

Answer: B - Price as premium device ($500-600) to establish new category
