Innovation Under Constraint: Reconfiguring Artificial Intelligence Systems through Complement-Oriented Strategies

Kate Jue Wang

Penn State University

Motivation: Firms focus on improving components when faced with constraint on technological components

Technology is a system of components (Arthur, 2009).

Constrains on one component can lower the performance of the technology, which creates a technological bottleneck (Rosenberg, 1976; Baldwin, 2020)

Studies on bottleneck strategies: firms would primarily focus on component-oriented innovations (Adner & Kapoor, 2010; Ethiraj, 2007)

Examples:

Tesla partner with Panasonic to develop in-house cell chemistries when bottlenecked by battery supply for their electronic cars

ASML concentrated on developing extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography when the semiconductor performance was bottlenecked by the size of transistor nodes

Lock-in problem of such component-oriented strategy

Firms become trapped in specific technological paths through self-reinforcing cycles that stabilize existing approaches (Leydesdorff, 2000)

Path-dependent resource allocation creates institutional routines and cognitive frameworks (Vanloqueren & Baret, 2009)

Firms create specialized capabilities and routines around bottleneck components that become difficult to abandon (Pentland et al., 2022)

The lock-in downside intensifies for emerging technologies

Emerging technologies are defined as “radically novel and relatively fast-growing technologies characterized by coherence over time, with the potential for substantial socio-economic impact, but accompanied by uncertainty and ambiguity.” (Rotolo, Hicks, and Martin, 2015: page 1828)

Bottlenecks are not easily identifiable

System architectures remain fluid, making it unclear which components will prove critical for long-term success

Research Question

How constrains on components of emerging technologies influence firms' innovation strategies?

Complement-oriented innovations

Innovating complementary components that can offset or absorb the constraints of the bottleneck component

Dimension Component-oriented innovations Complement-oriented innovations
Object of action The constrained component Complementary components that absorb or offset constraint
Assumption System architecture fixed; bottleneck indispensable Architecture fluid; systemic importance of components can be reweighted
Goal Remove or improve bottleneck Reduce bottleneck's systemic salience
Mechanism Acquisition, improvement, or coordination of bottleneck Rebalancing innovation portfolio toward substitutes, complements, or architectural alternatives
Outcome Restores existing system performance Redirects technological trajectory and avoids lock-in

Formal Model

Empirical Testing: AI firms from 2002-2018

AI has three complement components: data, algorithms, and computing power

Public firms can choose to innovate on data or complement algorithms and computing power (2002-2018, AI as emerging tech)

State regulation on data breach disclosure constrain access of data

California is the first state that enact data breach disclosure regulations in 2002, which requires organizations to reveal the data breach description, what data is breached.

By 2018, all 50 states have enacted similar data breach disclosure laws.

Final sample of public firm innovation

2027 firms with AI patents from 2002-2018

Key findings:

After state regulations on data breach disclosure, firms increase their complement-oriented innovations by 10%

One standard deviation increase of technological scope of firms, the impact of regulation of data breach disclosure increase by 5%.

One standard deviation increase of competitive intesity on data innovation, the impact of regulation of data breach disclosure increase by 15%.

H1: Regulation constrains on components of emerging technologies lead to complement-oriented innovations

Firms’ technological scope

Definition: broader technological experience (e.g., across multiple AI subfields) have richer cognitive maps of interdependencies

High technological scope -> firms' ability to seek alternative complement components and redesign the system

H2: Technological scope as a moderator

Competitive Intensity in the Constrained Component

Definition: the degree to which firms’ competitors concentrate their efforts on the constrained component

High competitive intensity -> firms' incentive to differentiate from competitors by focusing on complement components and system redesign

H3: Competitive intensity as a moderator

Conclusion

1. Data breach regulations lead to more complement-oriented innovations

2. Two essential boundary conditions: technological scope and competitive intensity

Contribution: explore the role of complement-oriented innovations

Prior Studies: when the components are the bottlenecks, firms would benefit by focusing on component-oriented innovations(Wu, Wan, and Levinthal, 2014)

This study: component-oriented innovations can cause lock-in effect for emerging technologies

Complement-oriented innovations mitigate lock-in effect and circumventing the bottleneck

Contribution: Generalist versus specialists

Prior Studies: debate on whether firms should be more generalists or specialists on developing emerging technologies(Hannan & Freeman 1977; Carroll & Swaminathan 2000; Brusoni, Prencipe & Pavitt 2001)

This study: broader technological experience can be better for emerging technologies when firms are faced with bottleneck on certain components
