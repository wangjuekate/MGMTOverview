What Works

  • Welcoming Environment: Instructor is friendly, inclusive, and creates a supportive atmosphere

What Doesn't Work

Areas Needing Improvement

  • Clarity: Instructions, assignment sheets, and syllabus are often vague
  • Structure: Discussions can feel unstructured; some students disengage
  • Communication: Delayed responses and unclear deadline changes
  • Lecture Design: Slides lack substance and visuals; hard to follow and take notes
  • Relevance: Unclear purpose of patent website; weak connections between simulations and learning goals
  • Receptivity: Occasional reports of dismissing student input
  • Engagement: Open-ended simulations can feel unfocused
  • Pacing: Jumping into material too quickly; insufficient depth on some topics

Assignments → Job Outcomes

Every assignment is a direct rehearsal for recruiting moments you will face within the next 3–6 months.

Treat each one as a job‑winning artifact you can show or speak to.

1) Blog Post & Discussion → Thought Leadership

  • What recruiters evaluate: clarity of thinking, business writing, and your opinions on tech & markets.
  • Job moment: “Walk me through your perspective on X trend.”
  • Deliverable you can share: polished post + comment thread that demonstrates insight and dialogue.
  • Resume/LinkedIn signal: “Published analysis on [topic]; engaged 10+ peer responses.”

➡ Outcome: you sound like a practitioner, not a student.

2) Simulation Reflection → Interview Story Bank

  • Mirrors real interview prompts: “Tell me about a time you handled a similar situation.”
  • Builds STAR answers (Situation–Task–Action–Result) with metrics you can reuse.
  • Job moment: behavioral & case interviews where you must show judgment under ambiguity.
  • Deliverable: 2–3 tight stories ready to narrate in first‑round and final‑round interviews.

➡ Outcome: confident, structured answers that land offers.

3) Product Website → Portfolio Proof

  • Real requirement: investors & hiring managers Google you; they should find a clean product page.
  • Shows: problem framing, value proposition, traction signals, and call‑to‑action.
  • Job moments: PM/Marketing/VC roles asking for a portfolio link or work sample.
  • Deliverable: live site you can place on resume/LinkedIn and email after a coffee chat.

➡ Outcome: a credible artifact that shortcuts “do you have evidence?”

4) Investor Pitch → Compelling Communication

  • Trains you to distill technical products into business value—fast.
  • Job moments: super‑days, case walk‑throughs, founder/BD calls.
  • Deliverable: 5–7 slide deck + 90‑second narrative you can repurpose for interviews.
  • Evaluated on: clarity, insight, and audience‑specific messaging.

➡ Outcome: you become someone people want to keep talking to.

5) In‑Class Discussion → On‑Your‑Feet Skills

  • Practice: active listening, executive brevity, structured pushback.
  • Job moments: panel interviews, group cases, client meetings.
  • Deliverable: real‑time reps with feedback you can implement next class/interview.
  • Recruiter tells: concise answers, crisp transitions, confident tone.

➡ Outcome: you speak like a teammate executives can trust.
