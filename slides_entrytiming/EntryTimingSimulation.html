Whether Helios & Matheson should enter the movie-ticketing market by acquiring MoviePass?

List on the board

What are First-Mover and Second-Mover Advantages faced by Helios (discussing on Appropriability; Uncertainty; Learning Effects; Network Effects & Switching Costs))?

CEO

Should Helios and Matheson Analytics acquire MoviePass in 2017?

CEO Key Takeaways

Strategic Considerations:

First-Mover Advantage: MoviePass has early market presence in subscription model

Learning Effects: Can HMA leverage data analytics expertise in new market?

Appropriability: How defensible is MoviePass's business model?

Strategic Fit: Exploit current technologies or seek to acquire new technologies

CFO

Question: How should Helios and Matheson Analytics handle its finance next year?


A: Adopt a cost cutting strategy and save as much cash as possible

B: Adopt an expansion strategy and sustain with borrowing from bank

CFO Takeaways

Financial Analysis Framework:

Financial Stability: Sufficient cash reserves, manageable debt levels required

Acquisition Costs: $12M convertible note + $5M promissory note

Revenue Stream Sustainability: Can MoviePass achieve profitability?

Risk Assessment: High cash burn rate vs. potential synergies

CMO

Question: How should Helios and Matheson Analytics adopt marketing strategy next year?

A: Prioritize MoviePass as the marketing goal

B: Prioritize Zone Technologies as the marketing goal

CMO Takeaways

Market Analysis Framework:

Market Uncertainty: Is subscription movie model sustainable?

Consumer Adoption: Rapid growth but unclear long-term retention

Network Effects: Does MoviePass create switching costs?

Competitive Dynamics: Theater chains could launch competing services

COO

Question: How should Helios and Matheson Analytics operate next year?

A: Adjust operation plans to fully utilize first mover advantages of MoviePass

B: Keep current operation plans and test water on MoviePass’ business

COO Takeaways

Operational Analysis Framework:

Revenue Sustainability: Subscription model vs. per-ticket costs

Cost Structure Scalability: Variable costs increase with usage

Scale Economies: Can HMA achieve operational efficiencies?

First-Mover Operational Risk: Unproven business model at scale

CHRO

Question: Who should Helios and Matheson Analytics hire next year?

A: Hire 80% social media marketing people

B: Hire 80% technology engineers

CHRO Takeaways

Human Capital Analysis:

Talent Acquisition: Consumer tech vs. B2B analytics expertise

Learning Curve: Can existing team adapt to consumer market?

Cultural Integration: Startup culture vs. analytics firm

Resource Allocation: Social media marketing vs. technology engineers

CTO

Question: How should Helios and Matheson Analytics focus their technology development next year?

A: Prioritize MoviePass’ AI project

B: Prioritize Zone Technologies’ mapping function

CTO Takeaways

Technology Analysis Framework:

Appropriability (Teece, 1986): Is MoviePass technology protected?

Technology Scalability: Can infrastructure handle rapid growth?

Complementary Assets: Theater partnerships critical for success

Competitive Threat: Easy for competitors to replicate app/model

Key Takeaways: Acquisition of MoviePass

Helios and Matheson Analytics shut down MoviePass in 2019

HMA went bankruptcy in 2020

Entry Timing Framework Applied:

First-Mover Advantage: MoviePass entered early but with unsustainable model

Appropriability (Teece, 1986): Weak - easy to replicate subscription model

Market Uncertainty: High - unclear if consumers would sustain subscriptions

Learning Effects: Limited - no steep learning curve in ticketing

Network Effects: Weak - no strong switching costs for consumers

Conclusion: Poor entry timing despite market excitement
