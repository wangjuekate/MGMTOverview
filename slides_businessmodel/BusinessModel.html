What is a Business Model?

A business model describes how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value.

Create Value

How you produce products/services

Deliver Value

How you reach customers

Capture Value

How you generate revenue

Key Elements of Business Models

Customer Segments

Who are your target customers?

Value Propositions

What value do you offer customers?

Customer Relationships

How do you interact with customers?

Revenue Streams

How do you make money?

Four Types of Business Models

Product Model

Create & sell physical/digital products

Pros:

  • Scalable production
  • Clear value proposition
  • Established market

Cons:

  • High inventory costs
  • Competition pressure
  • Limited differentiation

Example: Apple (iPhone), Target (retail)

Service Model

Provide expertise & labor-based solutions

Pros:

  • High margins
  • Custom solutions
  • Strong relationships

Cons:

  • Limited scalability
  • Talent dependent
  • Time-intensive

Example: McKinsey (consulting), Uber (rides)

Match-making Model

Connect buyers & sellers, take transaction fees

Pros:

  • Network effects
  • Low marginal costs
  • Scalable growth

Cons:

  • Chicken-egg problem
  • Trust & safety issues
  • Disintermediation risk

Example: Airbnb (hosts-guests), eBay (buyers-sellers)

Multisided Model

Serve multiple customer groups with cross-subsidization

Pros:

  • Multiple revenue streams
  • Strong network effects
  • High switching costs

Cons:

  • Complex coordination
  • Balancing act required
  • Regulatory challenges

Example: Google (users-advertisers), Fortnite (players-developers)

Key Takeaways

Four Models to Choose From

Product, Service, Match-making, and Multisided models each offer different approaches to creating and capturing value

Value Creation & Capture Determine Choice

Your approach to creating value and capturing revenue determines which business model fits best

Evaluate Market to Determine Fit

Analyze market conditions, customer needs, and competitive landscape to select the optimal model
