What is a Business Model?

A business model describes how an organization creates, delivers, and captures value.

Create Value

How you produce products/services

Deliver Value

How you reach customers

Capture Value

How you generate revenue

Key Elements of Business Models

Customer Segments

Who are your target customers?

Value Propositions

What value do you offer customers?

Customer Relationships

How do you interact with customers?

Revenue Streams

How do you make money?

Discussion Time: 10 minutes per company

Target's Business Model

Value Creation

  • Private label products
  • Designer partnerships
  • Supply chain efficiency
  • Store experience design

Value Delivery

  • Physical retail stores
  • Online platform
  • Mobile app
  • Same-day delivery

Value Capture

  • Revenue: Product sales, credit card fees
  • Cost Structure: Inventory, real estate, labor
  • Key Metrics: Same-store sales growth, inventory turnover

McKinsey's Business Model

Value Creation

  • Expert knowledge
  • Proprietary methodologies
  • Global talent network
  • Industry insights

Value Delivery

  • Client engagement teams
  • On-site consulting
  • Strategic recommendations
  • Implementation support

Value Capture

  • Revenue: Project fees, retainer agreements
  • Cost Structure: Consultant salaries, travel, research
  • Key Metrics: Utilization rates, client retention

Airbnb's Business Model

Value Creation

  • Platform connecting hosts & guests
  • Trust & safety systems
  • User experience design
  • Global marketplace

Value Delivery

  • Mobile & web platform
  • Search & booking system
  • Payment processing
  • Customer support

Value Capture

  • Revenue: Host service fees (3%), guest service fees (6-12%)
  • Cost Structure: Technology, marketing, operations
  • Key Metrics: Gross booking value, take rate

Fortnite's Business Model

Value Creation

  • Free-to-play game
  • Regular content updates
  • Social gaming experience
  • Cross-platform play

Value Delivery

  • Multiple gaming platforms
  • Battle royale gameplay
  • Creative mode
  • Live events

Value Capture

  • Revenue: In-game purchases (skins, battle pass)
  • Cost Structure: Game development, servers, marketing
  • Key Metrics: Monthly active users, average revenue per user

Business Model Innovation

Business model innovation can be more powerful than product innovation

Traditional Models

  • Product sales
  • Service fees
  • Licensing
  • Subscriptions

Innovative Models

  • Platform/marketplace
  • Freemium
  • Sharing economy
  • Ecosystem

Key Question: How can you create, deliver, or capture value differently?

Case Study: Netflix in 2007

The Challenge

Netflix is considering changes to their business model. They've just introduced instant-watching for PCs in January 2007.

Current Success

  • Largest online movie rental service
  • Rapid subscription growth
  • DVD-by-mail model

New Opportunity

  • Instant-watching technology
  • Streaming capabilities
  • Digital transformation

Question: How should Netflix evolve their business model?

Key Takeaways

Business Model Thinking

Always consider how you create, deliver, and capture value

Innovation Opportunity

Business model innovation can create competitive advantage

Customer Focus

Successful models align value creation with customer needs

Sustainability

Ensure your model can generate sustainable profits
