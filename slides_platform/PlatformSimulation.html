Simulation: Amazon Integrating API in 2002

Role: CEO

Should Amazon develop its API services? If so, how?

CEO Information

  • We and our sellers list millions of unique new, used and collectible items.
  • We also own and operate the Internet Movie Database (IMDb).
  • We offer e-commerce services to other businesses through our Merchant.com and Syndicated Stores programs.

Your task is to get input from your executive team to make a decision.

Role: CTO

What is Amazon's technological strategy for next year?

  • A: Amazon should focus on developing API for their merchants.
  • B: Amazon should focus on developing API for their consumers.

CTO Information & Answer

Our current strategy is to focus our development efforts on creating and enhancing the specialized, proprietary software that is unique to our business and to license or acquire commercially-developed technology for other applications where available and appropriate. Commissions received through our Amazon Marketplace and Merchants@ programs are recorded as net amounts since we are acting as an agent.

Answer: A - Focus on developing API for merchants to build on our platform.

Role: CMO

Amazon should adopt a market strategy as follows:

  • A: Amazon should advertise more to consumers about their amazon.com shopping.
  • B: Amazon should advertise more to merchants about their API services.

CMO Information & Answer

Our best marketing efforts are focused on improving the customer experience, which drives word-of-mouth promotion and repeat customer visits. Our business is generally affected by both seasonal fluctuations in Internet usage (which generally declines during summer months) and traditional retail seasonality.

Answer: B - Advertise more to merchants about their API services.

Role: COO

Which segment should Amazon integrate API services into?

  • A: Integrate in BMVD Segment and International Segment.
  • B: Integrate in ETK Segment and Services Segment.

COO Information & Answer

Our segments include: retail sales from www.amazon.com and www.amazon.ca of books, music and DVD/video products (BMVD); retail sales of electronics, home improvement and home and garden products (ETK); all retail sales of our internationally-focused Web sites; and commissions and fees from our services business.

Answer: B - Integrate in ETK Segment and Services Segment.

Role: CFO

What financial strategies should Amazon take?

  • A: Amazon should allocate more funding to the BMVD Segment and International Segment.
  • B: Amazon should allocate more funding to the ETK Segment and Services Segment.

CFO Information & Answer

Our BMVD segment had net sales of $1.87 billion in 2002. Our International segment had net sales of $1.17 billion. Our ETK segment had net sales of $645 million. Our results from operations were income of $64 million in 2002, compared to losses in prior years.

Answer: B - Allocate more funding to the ETK Segment and Services Segment.

Role: CHRO

What is Amazon's hiring strategy for next year?

  • A: Amazon should provide more salary payments to employees.
  • B: Amazon should provide more stock options to employees.

CHRO Information & Answer

We need to train and manage our employee base. Our migration to a technology platform that utilizes a less-costly technology infrastructure has improved expense management. Our current and planned personnel may not be adequate to support future operations.

Answer: B - Amazon should provide more stock options to employees.

Key Takeaways: Amazon's Platform Strategy

Strategic Alignment is Crucial:

  • Technology (CTO): The decision to build APIs for merchants was foundational to creating a platform.
  • Marketing (CMO): Shifting focus to attracting merchants was key to building the supply side of the platform.
  • Operations (COO): Targeting the right segments (ETK, Services) for API integration maximized impact.
  • Finance (CFO): Allocating capital to support the new platform strategy was essential for its success.
  • HR (CHRO): Using stock options aligned employee incentives with the long-term vision.

A successful platform strategy requires a coordinated effort across all functions of the organization.
