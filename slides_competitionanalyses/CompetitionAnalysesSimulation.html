Key Takeaways: Competitive Analysis in Action

Applying Porter's Five Forces to H&M's Strategy:

  • Rivalry Among Competitors: H&M chose to compete by strengthening its core competency (sustainable materials and design) rather than engaging in a tech race (Gen AI) with rivals like Shein or Zara. This is a move to differentiate rather than compete on the same terms.
  • Threat of New Technology (Substitutes): The decision to de-prioritize Gen AI (COO, CTO) shows an assessment that the threat from this new technology was less critical than strengthening their established value proposition.
  • Bargaining Power of Buyers: In an inflationary environment (CEO's context), buyers have high power. H&M's strategy to offer value through sustainability and quality design is a direct response to this pressure.
  • Internal Analysis (Resource-Based View): The decisions to fund design/manufacturing (CFO) and hire designers (CHRO) reflect a strategy to invest in internal, unique resources that are hard for competitors to imitate.

Effective competitive strategy involves analyzing the external environment (like rivals and new tech) and aligning it with your core internal strengths.
