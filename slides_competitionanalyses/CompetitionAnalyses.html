Porter's Five-Force Model

Degree of Existing Rivalry

  • Number of Competitors: Number of competitors in the industry
  • Industry Growth Rate: Slow growth intensifies competition for market share
  • Product Differentiation: Similar products increase price competition
  • Exit Barriers: High fixed costs and specialized assets trap firms in the industry
  • Strategic Stakes: High importance to firm strategy increases competitive intensity

Bargaining Power of Suppliers

  • Supplier Concentration: Few suppliers have more power to raise prices
  • Switching Costs: High costs of changing suppliers increase their power
  • Uniqueness of Input: Specialized or differentiated inputs increase supplier leverage
  • Forward Integration Threat: Suppliers' ability to enter your industry
  • Importance to Suppliers: If you're a small customer, suppliers have more power

Bargaining Power of Buyers

  • Buyer Concentration: Few large buyers have more negotiating power
  • Switching Costs: Low switching costs increase buyer power
  • Product Standardization: Commodity products give buyers more options
  • Backward Integration Threat: Buyers' ability to produce the product themselves
  • Price Sensitivity: Impact of product cost on buyer's total costs

Threat of Potential Entrants

  • Capital Requirements: High investment needs create barriers to entry
  • Economies of Scale: Cost advantages of existing large firms deter new entrants
  • Brand Loyalty: Strong customer loyalty makes entry difficult
  • Access to Distribution: Existing relationships with distributors block newcomers
  • Regulatory Barriers: Licenses, patents, and regulations limit entry
  • Retaliation: Expected aggressive response from incumbent firms

Threat of Substitutes

  • Relative Price-Performance: Substitutes offering better value threaten the industry
  • Switching Costs: Low costs to switch to substitutes increase the threat
  • Buyer Propensity: Customer willingness to try alternative solutions
  • Emerging Technologies: New technologies that fulfill the same need differently
  • Complementary Products: Products that reduce need for the original offering

Key Takeaways

🎯 Porter's Five Forces Framework:

  • Systematic Analysis: Provides a structured approach to assess industry attractiveness
  • Five Key Forces: Rivalry, supplier power, buyer power, new entrants, and substitutes
  • Strategic Positioning: Understanding forces helps identify competitive advantages

💡 Business Applications:

  • Industry Selection: Evaluate attractiveness before entering new markets
  • Competitive Strategy: Position your firm to defend against strong forces
  • Dynamic Analysis: Forces change over time - continuous monitoring is essential

🚀 Remember:

The goal is not just to understand the forces, but to shape them in your favor!
