Kate Wang - Penn State University

Net Present Value (NPV)

Required for estimating project value over 5-10 years. Key components include:

  • EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes)
  • Accountant Adjustment (to find Free Cash Flow)
  • Discount Factor (WACC)
  • Termination Value

EBIT: Earnings Before Interest and Taxes

Gross Income

  • Revenue from operations (sales)
  • Income from fees/advertisement

All Expenses

  • Cost of material
  • Cost of power/fuel/etc.
  • Cost of employees

Operating Profit = Gross Income - All Expenses - Depreciation

Accountant Adjustment to find Free Cash Flow (FCFF)

FCFF adjusts EBIT for non-cash items and investments:

  • Tax: Adjust for the corporate tax rate.
  • Non-cash Expenses: Add back depreciation.
  • Working Capital: Adjust for changes in inventory and other working capital items.
  • Capital Expenditures: Subtract investments in long-term assets (plant, machinery).

Discount Factor: WACC

\[ WACC = \left( \frac{E}{V} \cdot R_e \right) + \left( \frac{D}{V} \cdot R_d \cdot (1-T_c) \right) \]

\[ \text{Discount factor} = \frac{1}{(1 + \text{WACC })^{n}} \]

E = Market value of equity, D = Market value of debt, V = Total value (E+D), Re = Cost of Equity, Rd = Cost of Debt, Tc = Corporate Tax Rate.

Terminal Value

Estimates the value of the project beyond the explicit forecast period.

\[ \text{Terminal Cash Flow} = \text{Free Cash Flow}_{t-1} \times (1 + g) \]

\[ \text{Terminal Value} = \frac{\text{Terminal Cash Flow}}{\text{WACC} - g} \]

'g' is the perpetual growth rate, often based on industry average or inflation.

Key Takeaways: Financial Evaluation Concepts

Core Principles:

  • NPV: A fundamental tool for assessing long-term project profitability.
  • EBIT & FCFF: Start with earnings, then adjust for non-cash items and investments to find the actual cash available.
  • WACC: The discount rate must reflect the project's specific risk profile and capital structure.

Application Insights:

  • Forecasting is key; assumptions about growth, costs, and risk drive the final valuation.
  • Terminal value often represents a significant portion of the total NPV, making the perpetual growth rate a critical assumption.

A rigorous evaluation provides a framework for decision-making, but it's only as good as its underlying assumptions.
